App mockup, round 1

Clickable sketch of the four surfaces we agreed. Tap presets, drag the volume, switch the viewer between Stefan and Kay to see per-person preset ordering. The app is deliberately dark-first, like the gear it controls.

Main screen

The daily driver: one tap to a preset.

Volume-26.0 dB
  • Presets show source + rooms; the live strip reflects reality even if someone used the MusicCast app.
  • Each preset has its own colour, carried through to the now-playing dot and the Watch.
  • The strip's button is mute, not play/pause: it works identically for every source (including Decks and Apple TV) and mutes all the preset's rooms at once. True pause stays with the system's own controls where they exist.
  • Reordering: long-press a tile enters wiggle mode (Home Screen convention), drag to your order. Order is per-person; definitions and baselines are shared.
  • Volume is universal across the active preset's rooms, shown in dB; per-room baselines sit behind it.
  • The tick under the slider marks the active preset's baseline; firing a preset returns volume to it. Drag away freely; long-press the slider to save the current level as the baseline (the tick moves to you).
  • Type is large and heavy throughout, with contrast beyond WCAG AAA (white on near-black, dark ink on bright tiles).
